Philosophy

Great architects approach each building as a total work of art, where every part is tuned to contribute to the whole â€“  down to the last doorknob. The same is true of filmmakers. Every second of runtime will either reinforce or undermine a film's core message. There is no room for technical errors: audio must be clean and well mixed; exposure must be correct; and color must be consistent. Taken together, every part must serve the whole from the composition of every shot to the rate of cutting, to the sweep of the music. The mastery of filmmaking, then, is the ability to focus on each aspect of the film without occluding the total view of their ensemble.
